The philosophy
of Ikigai

Ikigai (生き甲斐) is a Japanese concept meaning "reason for being." It describes the convergence of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for.

In Okinawa — one of the world's Blue Zones — residents attribute their extraordinary longevity not just to diet, but to having a clear Ikigai. Purpose, it turns out, is life-giving.

生き甲斐

What you Love 情熱

Your passions — activities that energise you, that you'd do without pay. Without it, a career becomes a cage.

What you're Good At 使命

Your natural strengths. Being good at something you love turns effort into flow — hours vanish.

What the World Needs 天職

Your mission. Careers that serve a genuine need create lasting impact and lasting employment.

What you're Paid For 職業

Sustainable livelihood. Ikigai isn't about ignoring money — it's making purpose and paycheck point together.
